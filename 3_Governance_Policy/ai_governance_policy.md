## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
This AI Governance Policy establishes the framework for responsible development, deployment, and monitoring of AI systems at DiagnosAI Sweden AB. It ensures compliance with the EU AI Act and promotes trustworthy AI practices.

### Scope
This policy applies to all AI systems developed or deployed by DiagnosAI Sweden AB, all employees and contractors involved in AI development, and all AI systems interacting with patient data.

---

## 2. AI GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES

| Principle | Definition | EU AI Act Reference |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Transparent | AI decisions can be explained to users | Article 13 |
| Human-overseen | Humans can review, override and correct AI | Article 14 |
| Accurate | AI performs reliably for its intended purpose | Article 15 |
| Fair | AI does not discriminate across patient groups | Article 10 |
| Safe | AI risks are identified and mitigated | Article 9 |
| Accountable | Decisions are logged and traceable | Article 12 |

---

## 3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### AI Governance Committee
Members: CEO, CTO, Chief Medical Officer, Data Protection Officer, AI Compliance Officer  
Meets: Quarterly  
Responsibilities: Review and approve AI governance policy, oversee compliance, review incident reports.

### AI Compliance Officer
Responsibilities: Maintain AI system inventory, conduct compliance assessments, manage technical documentation, report to AI Governance Committee.

### AI Development Team
Responsibilities: Implement technical requirements, document AI systems, implement logging systems, conduct bias assessments.

### Clinical Users (Physicians)
Responsibilities: Receive AI literacy training, exercise independent clinical judgment, report anomalies or concerns.

---

## 4. AI SYSTEM LIFECYCLE GOVERNANCE

### Phase 1: Concept and Risk Classification
- Define intended purpose
- Classify AI system under EU AI Act
- Conduct initial risk assessment

### Phase 2: Development
- Implement risk management system
- Prepare technical documentation
- Implement logging capabilities
- Design human oversight interface

### Phase 3: Pre-Deployment Approval
- Compliance assessment sign-off
- Technical documentation review
- Staff training completed

### Phase 4: Deployment and Monitoring
- Monthly performance monitoring
- Quarterly bias audits
- Annual full risk re-assessment

### Phase 5: Decommissioning
- Archive technical documentation
- Data deletion according to GDPR

---

## 5. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

A serious incident is any malfunction that results in patient harm, incorrect clinical decision, data breach, or extended system unavailability.

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Immediately | Notify AI Compliance Officer |
| Within 24 hours | Notify national authority if required |
| Within 2 weeks | Root cause analysis complete |
| Within 1 month | Corrective action implemented |

---

## 6. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

| Role | Training Required | Frequency |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| All staff | AI literacy basics | Annually |
| Clinical users | AI system operation and limitations | Before use plus annually |
| Development team | EU AI Act technical requirements | Annually |

Key Performance Indicators:

- Clinical staff training completion: target 100%
- Incident reports filed within 24h: target 100%
- Monthly performance reports completed: target 100%
